How our
service works

We are a mental health service that leverages on innovative tools and mobile technology to provide free mental health screening for Ghanaians and facilitate the provision of free/affordable care for them under the NHIS.


We have a toll free USSD/Short code ( *844*555# ) which users dial and provide responses to some short questions. User responses are instantly relayed to our call center where personnel call users to screen them further. A free/subsidized (NHIS supported) consultation at the nearest mental unit is clinched for users who are found to have symptoms suggestive of a mental health disorder.

*Disclaimer: We neither diagnose nor offer definitive management of mental health disorders.

OUR STORY

BY FOUNDER (DR. ATSU LATEY)

MindIT was borne out of a teacherâ€™s plight for help. In commemoration of World Health Day 2017, the founder and CEO of MindIT, Dr Atsu Latey (then a 5th year medical student who had just returned from a Psychiatry elective in New York University) featured an article in a local newspaper on how to recognize depression in people. A teacher from a remote town in Ghana read the piece and identified herself as being depressed and so she called him for help. She did not know she was depressed, she did not know there was treatment available and she also did not know where she could get the help from. She was importunate with her requests but unfortunately, he got out of touch with her and did not hear from her again. It dawned on him that there may be some people who may be suffering the same predicament as her and so he thought of a way to screen the Ghanaian population for mental illness and offer them care which is affordable and closest to them. Fast forward, MindIT was born from a group of youth who believed they could provide their skills pro bono to develop innovative IT tools to provide affordable and accessible mental healthcare to Ghanaians.

Dr. Atsu Latey

THE RAFT

ABOUT THE RAFT

In the context of poor funding for mental healthcare, which is punctuated by the inacessibility and cost nature of care, it is imperative that cost-efficient soluntions are developed to address the burgeoning needs of mental healthcare in Ghana. This is one of the main aims of MindIT Ghana, as we contribute our skills pro bono to provide affordable and accesible mental healthcare for Ghanaians.
